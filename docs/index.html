The three-stage mechanism

Runs inside any Uniswap v4 pool created with a dynamic LP fee. No off-chain components. No oracles. Pure stateless-per-swap bookkeeping.

01

Same-block detection

beforeAddLiquidity flags any position added in the same block as the previous swap on that pool. The position is time-locked from earning the surge premium for the next 5 blocks. Passive LPs are unaffected; JIT searchers are not.

02

Surge premium

When a flagged position exits during its lock window, the next swap on the pool is charged a 0.7% surge fee on top of the base LP fee. The surge is paid by the swapper, not the LP. 90% of the surge premium goes to passive LPs as compensation.

03

Protocol cut

The remaining 10% of the surge premium is carved out via BeforeSwapDelta and pulled into the hook contract through PoolManager.take. This is the protocol's revenue line — sustaining the project without taxing honest activity.

Verify it yourself

Every claim on this page can be verified from a public RPC. Paste these three commands — no account, no API key — and watch the chain confirm the hook holds the tokens it earned. 

# 1. Hook says it accrued 7×10¹⁴ wei of currency0:
cast call 0xca2f96f95c9E7a2109ecbD64bf80F7Cf77d86ac8 \
    'accruedFees(address)(uint256)' \
    0x7CE8535D4fD28DE2d929B03A7B6d9d6045F36872 \
    --rpc-url https://ethereum-sepolia.publicnode.com
# → 700000000000000

# 2. The currency0 contract confirms the hook actually holds those tokens:
cast call 0x7CE8535D4fD28DE2d929B03A7B6d9d6045F36872 \
    'balanceOf(address)(uint256)' \
    0xca2f96f95c9E7a2109ecbD64bf80F7Cf77d86ac8 \
    --rpc-url https://ethereum-sepolia.publicnode.com
# → 700000000000000

# 3. Hook config — what it will do on the NEXT swap:
cast call 0xca2f96f95c9E7a2109ecbD64bf80F7Cf77d86ac8 \
    'surgeFeeBips()(uint24)' \

The two balances match. The hook's internal bookkeeping is exactly equal to its real ERC20 balance. That is the end-to-end proof: the mechanism does not just emit events; it accumulates a tradeable balance.

For pool deployers

Building a new dynamic-fee v4 pool? Attach JITShield as the hook. Your pool inherits MEV protection for LPs and a transparent surge-fee mechanism — without adding any operational overhead. The hook is permissionless. You don't ask anyone.

  • Single hook address; passes v4 permission validation
  • No oracle. No off-chain keeper. No external dependency.
  • Open MIT source; you can fork, audit, or fork-and-modify
  • Initial config tunable per deployment

For LPs

Provide passive liquidity into a JITShield-protected pool. Your range stays earning base fees as normal. When a JIT searcher tries to extract from a swap, the hook injects a surge fee that is redistributed to honest LPs — you get more than the base fee on shielded swaps, automatically.

  • No new contracts to interact with — standard v4 modify-liquidity flow
  • No subscription. No claim step. The fee just lands.
  • You see surge events on-chain — verify directly from Etherscan
